# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Quality engineering exists to prevent harm — defective products cause safety incidents, warranty costs, and supply chain failures. The standards this project implements (ISO 9001, IATF 16949, AIAG-VDA FMEA, VDA 6.3) exist because lives and organisations depend on getting methodology right.

We hold ourselves to the same standard in this community: rigorous, honest, and accountable.

We pledge to make participation in Quality-Engineering-Skills a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected behaviour

- **Methodology accuracy first.** If you identify an error in a skill, raise it with a standard reference and clause number. "This is wrong" is not a report — "AIAG PPAP 4th Ed Table 4.1 requires X, this says Y" is.
- **Cite your sources.** Claims about standard requirements must reference the edition. The quality discipline is precise about this; this community is too.
- **Constructive technical disagreement.** Practitioners disagree on methodology. Debate the content, not the person. A dissenting view with evidence is valuable; a dismissal without basis is not.
- **Respect domain expertise.** Contributors span ISO 9001 generalists to IATF 16949 automotive specialists to AS9100 aerospace practitioners. Expertise in one area does not invalidate expertise in another.
- **Acknowledge corrections gracefully.** The standards evolve; practitioners evolve. Being corrected is how accuracy improves.
- **Protect confidentiality.** Quality incidents shared as examples to improve a skill may involve real supplier or OEM situations. Do not share or identify specific companies, incidents, or individuals without permission.

### Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks in any form
- Dismissing contributions because of the contributor's role, seniority, or industry sector
- Publishing false methodology claims and refusing to correct them when cited evidence is provided
- Misrepresenting standard requirements — stating something is required by ISO 9001 or IATF 16949 when it is not
- Using this project's content in contexts that misrepresent its scope (e.g., claiming AI-generated outputs based on these skills constitute certified audit evidence)
- Doxing or publishing others' private information
- Other conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional quality engineering environment

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders — currently @RBraga01 as lead — are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to behaviour they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces — GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, code review comments — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces (e.g., LinkedIn posts referencing Quality-Engineering-Skills, conference presentations, community meetups).

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project lead at **qessupport@gmail.com**.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project lead is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

### Enforcement guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines in determining the consequence for any action deemed in violation of this Code of Conduct:

**1. Correction**
*Community impact:* Use of inappropriate language or other behaviour deemed unprofessional.
*Consequence:* A private written warning, clarifying the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behaviour was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**2. Warning**
*Community impact:* A violation through a single incident or series of actions.
*Consequence:* A warning with consequences for continued behaviour. No interaction with the people involved — including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct — for a specified period of time.

**3. Temporary ban**
*Community impact:* A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour.
*Consequence:* A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time.

**4. Permanent ban**
*Community impact:* Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour, harassment, or aggression toward individuals.
*Consequence:* A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.
